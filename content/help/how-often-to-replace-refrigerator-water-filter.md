# How often to replace a refrigerator water filter

There is no single calendar that fits every household. Use the interval below as a starting point, then adjust for how you actually use the fridge.

## Typical guidance

Many manufacturers suggest replacing the cartridge about **every six months** in average use. That is a convenience rule of thumb, not a law of physics.

## When to replace sooner

Consider changing the filter if you notice:

- **Slower flow** from the dispenser than usual  
- **Off taste or smell** in water or ice  
- A **filter reminder** on the display (after you confirm it is not a false alarm—see your manual)

Homes with heavy dispenser use, very hard water, or known water quality issues may need shorter intervals.

## When stretching slightly may be OK

Light dispenser use and stable, good-tasting water sometimes mean you can go a bit longer—still within what your manual allows. If unsure, follow the manufacturer’s stated maximum.

## After you replace

Run several gallons of water through the new cartridge (dispense and discard) so loose carbon fines clear and taste normalizes. Reset any filter indicator if your model has one.

---

*Your owner’s manual or the filter packaging is the best source for the interval your engineer intended for that specific design.*
