# How to find your refrigerator model number

You need the full model number (not the serial number) to look up the right water filter.

## Where to look

1. **Inside the fridge**  
   Open the fresh food compartment. On many units the tag is on a side wall, ceiling, or behind a drawer frame. Use good light; the stamp can be small.

2. **On the door frame**  
   Some models put the rating plate on the frame that the door closes against (often the upper hinge side).

3. **Behind the kick plate**  
   If you do not see a tag inside, check the toe grille or kick panel at the bottom front. It may snap or unscrew off.

4. **Paperwork**  
   The same model number usually appears on your purchase documents or on the manufacturer’s product registration.

## What to write down

- Copy the **model** exactly as printed (letters, numbers, and dashes).
- Do not confuse it with the **serial** number; filters are matched to the model.

## If the tag is worn off

Use any legible fragment and your brand name, or check the manual PDF on the manufacturer’s support site using the serial number—they often list the model there.

---

*Exact label location varies by brand and year. Your owner’s manual’s “finding model number” section is the definitive guide for your unit.*
